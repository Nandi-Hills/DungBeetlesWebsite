// The Worldview

THE MICRO
BATTLEFIELD

Welcome to the chaotic, vibrant, nature-inspired world of Dung Beetles. You don't control supersoldiers — you control determined, resilient scavengers surviving in an oversized world.

Two factions clash across a nature-scale arena. Every blade of grass is a wall. Every pebble is a fortress. Every pile of dung is victory itself.

The core fantasy lives in agility, instinct, and teamwork — and the chaos of competing for your most precious resource.

// Roster

SIX ROLES,
INFINITE PLAY

Role 01

Defender

Masters of territory. Defenders anchor your base perimeter, manage turret deployment, and intercept enemy carriers with precision knockback.

Role 02

Attacker

Born to disrupt. Attackers slash into enemy formations, strip carried dung from opponents, and create chaos that turns the tide of any escort.

Role 03

Scout

Information is the ultimate weapon. Scouts reveal threats before they manifest, track enemy positioning, and open flanking lanes through dark terrain.

Role 04

Trapper

Patience is a weapon. Trappers lay hidden snares along choke points, redirect enemy traffic, and create devastating kill zones around objectives.

Role 05

Stealth

Unseen. Unstoppable. Stealth beetles dissolve into shadow, slip through enemy lines undetected, and strike the moment their guard drops.

Role 06

Storm

Elemental fury in an exoskeleton. Storm beetles generate electromagnetic pulses that disrupt enemy abilities and shatter dung rolls on impact.

// Resource Economy

ONE RESOURCE.
THREE PURPOSES.

Dung is not just what you're fighting for — it's everything. It's your score, your ammunition, and your wallet. Every gram forces a strategic decision.

  • Victory Points — Bank at your home base to climb toward the target score.
  • Ability Fuel — Spend stored dung to power ranged attacks and special abilities.
  • Base Currency — Invest in turrets, walls, observation beetles, and trap networks.
// Design Philosophy OBJECTIVE
OVER KILL

Combat exists purely to interrupt enemy carriers, protect allies, reveal threats, and punish overextension. Knocking out an opponent simply forces them to drop their dung and respawn — creating tactical openings, not kill streaks.
